What putter does Wyndham Clark use?

Wyndham At Port Royal Golf Club, Clark won the Bermuda Championship with a thrilling final round to claim his first PGA Tour triumph. Before Brian Gay caught up to him late and forced a playoff, Clark produced a final-round 65 that included seven birdies in his opening 11 holes. Clark ultimately won the playoff.

Clark, a high school basketball teammate of Christian McCaffery, performed much as the NFL great usually does on Sundays. Clark nailed 17 of 18 greens in regulation thanks to good driving and an almost faultless iron game. Given many chances, Clark converted some of them, notably a 20-footer on hole 11 (a left-to-right snapshot that caught the right edge) to increase his lead to three shots at the time.

PXG’s 0811X+ with a Mitsubishi Diamana 60x shaft is Clark’s driver. The adjustable hosel is configured to flatten the lying angle and reduce the loft to 9 degrees. In order to create a draw bias, Clark additionally places the heavier movable weights in the heel area. Clark stated to Golf Digest in 2019 that the weights “assist because I tend to drift the ball.” It flattens that fading, making my misses far less frequent. In terms of driving distance for the week, Clark came in second with 310.5 yards.

American professional golfer Wyndham Clark was born in Denver, Colorado on December 9, 1993. He competes on the PGA Tour right now.

What putter does Wyndham Clark use?

His putter is the Gunboat mallet model from PXG, and his irons are the 0211FB prototypes with Nippon Modus 130X shafts and Golf Pride Tour Velvet cord grips.
