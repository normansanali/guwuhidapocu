Early life and education

Kelly spent her entire childhood in New Jersey together with her parents and has an 11-months younger sister Kathleen who is teaching math in a high school – she can rarely be heard talking about her family as she prefers not to get them involved in media. Kelly is of Irish-Italian and American nationality.

Kelly has been interested in dancing since she was five years old, when she began learning River Dance, a traditional Irish dance.  She would go on to practice dancing for the next 10 years, and perform all around New Jersey and New York, which made her somewhat popular in high school. After she matriculated, Kelly enrolled at Rutgers University from which she graduated with a bachelor’s degree in meteorology – during her time there, Kelly was a member of the Rutgers Meteorologist Club.

As Kelly wanted to further work on her education, she enrolled at the Middlesex County College and became an Associate of Science in Mathematics.

Career of a meteorologist

Kelly launched her career soon after she obtained her bachelor’s degree in meteorology, as she started working for WWOR-TV network as their intern in 2011. The network decided not to keep her after she finished her internship, and Kelly moved to Cheyenne in Wyoming and began working for the CBS News network.

Kelly did not stay long with CBS News as she switched to WSAZ-TV in 2013, where she became somewhat recognized as a meteorologist. She was part of the WSAZ-TV team for a year, working as their weather broadcaster before she moved to Boston and began working for the WCVB station – she can be seen reporting every day in the “Storm Team 5” show. Kelly’s career and her skills at broadcasting have won her an award – the Best Weather Broadcaster Award in 2013 for her coverage of the Colorado floods.
