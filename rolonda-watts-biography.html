Rolonda Watts Bio (Wiki)

Who is Rolonda Watts?

Rolonda Watts is an American actress, producer, voice-over artist, novelist, motivational speaker, and television and radio talk show host who is currently an on-camera announcer for ‘Divorce Court’.

Watts is known for her work as a host of ‘Eponymous Rolonda’.

What are the birth facts of Rolonda Watts?

Rolonda Watts was born on July 12, 1959, in Winston-Salem, North Carolina, United States of America. Her nationality is American and her ethnicity is mixed (African-American).

She was born to Dr. Velma Gibson Watts(mother), an associate dean at Wake Forest University and Roland Watts(father), chairman of Winston-Salem State University’s Fine Arts Department.

She was raised in the same place where she was born. Later, she moved to Atlanta, Georgia, and Columbia.

Education History

Talking about the educational background of Rolonda Watts, she firstly joined Salem Academy in Winston-Salem, North Carolina, and moved to  Spelman College in Atlanta.

After she graduated from Spelman College, she applied and joined Columbia University Graduate School of Journalism to gain a master’s degree in 1981.

Rolonda Watt: Professional Life, Career

During her days at Spelman College, she worked as an editor-in-chief for the school newspaper which is considered her first job.

After she graduated from Columbia University Graduate School of Journalism, she worked for ‘WFMY-TV’ as a general assignment reporter which was followed by her work at ‘WNBC’.

One year later, she worked for her talk show and took her step to be an actress in ‘Sister, Sister’ as Vivica Shaw. Since then, she played in numerous films and television shows like ‘The West Wing, ‘JAG’, ‘Lie Detector’, ‘The Bold and the Beautiful, etc.

Currently, she is the CEO of her own production company, Watts Works Productions and with the help of her talent and appearances, she is one of the most demanded television personalities with a decent amount of money.
