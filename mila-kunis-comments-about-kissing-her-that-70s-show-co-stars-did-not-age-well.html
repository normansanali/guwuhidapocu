Mila Kunis Said She Had No Choice But To Kiss Everyone On That '70s Show In What Was A Forgettable Interview

Looking back at forgettable interviews, this one in particular, alongside Craig Kilborn, might rank up there among her very worst ever. Mila Kunis was fresh into her career and on the verge of becoming a star. Instead of celebrating that fact, she was asked about kissing several of her That '70s Show co-stars. At that point, Topher Grace was the only cast member she didn't kiss. Given what came to light with Danny Masterson, her response was that much more alarming.

She said, "I kissed the Mastersons, I've kissed everybody. It is quite wrong." Kunis continues about the wrtiers on the show, "I don't know what they're thinking, I just have no choice but to kiss every guy on the show."

Danny Masterson Made A Disturbing Bet After Ashton Kutcher Kissed Mila Kunis For The First Time

Following Danny Masterson' guilty verdict, several interviews from the past started to resurface. One of them featured Masterson betting Ashton $10 to French kiss an underage Mila Kunis.

Kutcher revealed years ago in 2002, “She was 14 when we started the show. I was 19. And they were like, ‘You guys are going to be making out in this scene.’ And I’m thinking, ‘Wait, this is slightly illegal.'”

Kunis adds, “I was a 14-year-old little girl and I was extremely scared for my life. He was very nice about it, like, ‘Don’t worry about it.’ Then Danny goes to him and goes, ‘Dude, I’ll give you $10 if you French kiss her.'”

The bet was also confirmed by Kutcher himself.

“We had a little side bet going,” Kutcher confirms. “You’re kissing on the show, we’re boyfriend and girlfriend. You would use tongue. Danny bet me like 20 bucks I wouldn’t do it.”

Ashton Kutcher Admitted That Working With Mila Kunis Was Awkward

Kissing several of her co-stars was awkward for Mila Kunis, but the same held true for Ashton Kutcher during That '70s Show, especially given the age of his co-star. Kutcher revealed with CinemaBlend that working with Kunis on That '70s Show was a lot more awkward than That '90s Show.

Ashton revealed, "I mean, when we were there [on That ‘70s Show], she was 15 years old, I was 20 years old; there was zero romantic connection between the two of us. So, to go back and have it be this new shared experience was really wild and nostalgic. There’s so many memories that come alive, like when you see the basement again or these characters that were so much a part of our life for such a long time. It was just so nostalgic for us. I’m excited for these kids that are hopefully going to experience the same ride we got to go on when we were their age."

He continued, "Any day I get to go to work with my wife is the best day of the year. I think the crazy thing about that experience is we are in a completely different place in our relationship now than where we were then."

Without a doubt, fans would be open to seeing the two reunite once again on That '90s Show.
